A Good profession for a balance druid to have is tailoring for the enchants that they can weave on their cloak ie Lightweave Embroidery (embroiders a subtle pattern of light into your cloak, giving you a chance to increase your Intellect by 580 for 15 sec when casting a spell) or if you have a resto offspec then Darkglow Embroidery is good (embroiders a magical pattern into your cloak, giving you a chance to increase your Spirit by 580 for 15 sec when you cast a spell), tailors can also make Powerful Enchanted Spellthread (Permanently embroiders spellthread into pants, increasing Intellect by 95 and Stamina by 80) which is good for balance druids, they can also make other spellthreads as well.
With regards main hand enchant i have seen alot of balance druids with the Power Torrent enchant (Permanently enchant a weapon to sometimes increase Intellect by 500 for 12 sec when dealing damage or healing with spells), or the Hurricane enchant (Permanently enchant a melee weapon to sometimes increase haste rating by 450 for 12 sec when healing or dealing spell or melee damage).
Mastery isnt one of our most important stats at this moment in time, as of this moment in time our stat priority is as follows:-
Intellect, Spellpower, Hit/Spirit, Haste, Mastery and Crit this is going along with what Elitistjerks has said and most balance druids will swear by. Their website is always a good point of reference to check out with regards specs and updates, with regards raiding you do want to get your hit to 17% soft hit cap, once you have done that then reforge items to haste.
The trinkets i am currently using are Darkmoon : Volcano and the Stump of Time as when they proc it gives your spellpower a huge boost.
With regards what you have said Elorya i dont know if this has helped or you wanted someone more experienced to help, i hope this has helped a little, obviously make sure that your reputation is at exalted with the different factions so you can get the enchants that are available from them.
