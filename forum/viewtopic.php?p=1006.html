Me being annoyed has nothing whatsoever to do with your nationality or anything else, it was mainly the initial attitude which I thought you had.

On the +hit issue, slap an orange or yellow gem anywhere that makes you go over the 7% mark and gives you a socket bonus. It is going to be the absolute best gem in that slot, ever, if you're below the hit cap and can utilize all (or most) of the hit rating. If you run 10-mans that aren't sure to have a draenei in them, going even to 8% isn't bad by any means.

I've never personally had a rotation set in stone that I'd use - it's more of a priority on skill usage. So many things can and will happen in encounters that following a set rotation can hurt your DPS quite a bit. Obviously, it looks like the rotation you posted most of the time, but there's small things like delaying your skills a bit and dumping away an extra death coil if your RP would completely overflow from the next attack.

The other part that DKs in general are doing very poorly are using the cooldown skills. Gargoyle you should put up pretty much instantly when you have your procs on, so that you get to pop it again later on in the fight. You might want to delay it a bit if you're getting Heroism right away at the start. Always pop Army of the Dead pre-pull, macro your pet attack key with "pet: passive, pet:attack" so that they aren't taunting random crap. Keep using your AMS in every fight on stuff that you can get runic power out of, unless you need it for survival. Same with Empowered Rune Weapon - you usually get it once per fight. Save it for a time that you have some +dmg modifiers through the encounter mechanics (such as Blood Queen) or just pop it at the start when you have your trinkets rolling (so that you get to do it again later on).

Other parts that are quite obvious are using flasks and potions, but that you seem to have covered. You could also start popping armor potions pre-pull - that way you get the extra +AP going into the fight and you can still use a haste potion sometime during the fight.


Have you got any WMO-logs or anything else that you can link to see what exactly is happening during the fights where you're not putting out enough damage?
