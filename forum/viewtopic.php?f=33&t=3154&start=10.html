Reckert,

First of all I would like to apologize for criticizing you. My intention was simply to clarify what I had previously stated.

As far as your question goes, I'll share what works best from personal experience, but I will leave it up to you to figure out what works best. Data can now prove that the longer ignite spends ticking before it is hit again the less powerful it will be. Allowing ignite to tick for 2 seconds before it is hit again will result in a much weaker ignite than if the same two spells doing the same damage were to hit .1 seconds apart from eachother.

I've come to think at this point that the distance from the boss doesn't affect what you do. Wait for an ignite to be on the target, cast one more Fireball and then .1 seconds after it finishes casting you launch the Pyroblast. The only problem is you won't know if the fireball launched crit or not. Casting Living Bomb or Scorch will work in allowing you to see if Fireball has crit or not, but from my experience it causes ignite to be much smaller once the Pyroblast hits.

If you find different results or come up with something else, let me know. I'm always curious to find ways to improve myself.

-Guromin
