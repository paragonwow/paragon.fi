Basic start is PS-IT-BS-Blood tap-OB-OB. From there on out there is no rotation, it's a system of priorities. You keep the diseases up without letting them fall off for an extended period of time and smash away. RP overflow should be avoided; just dump a DC prior to it and delay a strike.

Hi, and thanks.

Usually, the parses that have stupid high DPS numbers in them include a bunch of buffs from other people. Stacking Hysteria and whatnot on one guy - this is quite evident if you browse some of the top DPS'ers. Sometimes they pad the stats with tons of useless extra AoE effects simply for bigger numbers. Those are the fights you should ignore and try to find one without such stuff happening. Having a very long fight length might also bring down your DPS somewhat, or having the fight end just before you get your cooldowns up again.

I don't really see anything 'wrong' in your Festergut fight. Just a little bit too little of AMS usage and only one blood tap - neither will jump your DPS by a ton, though.

Something similar for comparison: http://www.wowmeteronline.com/combat/11837969#damageout

At that point, I was still using T9 as I didn't have the badges to go for 4xT10. Same deal here - only one blood tap used, which was a bit of a fail. The main difference between me and the others is that I'm using the Glyph of Disease. Liha and Devah outgear me pretty heavily (and even more back during that kill). [edit:] Seems like Devah was trying out the GoD glyph on that particular kill too for the first time since the nerf.

GoD is still, by far, the best glyph you can choose. For one, you don't have to have a separate AoE spec anymore as you, practically, lose nothing of value. The good part comes from rolling buffs/debuffs through refreshing diseases. At worst, the glyph is about equal DPS to the second best glyph and will only get better as SS starts hitting for more. You also don't clip your diseases when using it.

GoD has been the single best glyph since release. The nerf to it hasn't changed the value much. Prior to nerf, you could apply diseases with all of the procs available and diseases would roll with that modifier as long as you kept refreshing them with GoD. Basically, whatever your modifiers etc were at that point, your diseases would inherit. This was changed in a recent patch, but only for the AP modifier. You still retain every single bit of +crit, +%dmg and such.

What all of the above means is you can roll the same extra +15% damage from Tricks of the Trade throughout the fight. Or you can fish for Cinderglacier procs if you swap a weapon - in some fights on some parts, you can easily afford the 2x1.5s weapon swap and still come out hugely on top (after all, you get +20% disease damage for the whole rest of the fight). This only gets better and better on fights with multiple targets [edit:] (the buffed up diseases proc higher damage wandering plague) or in fights such as Twin Val'kyrs - you can roll the +100% dmg buff throughout rest of the fight. It works pretty good with trinket procs as well, as your current AP gets applied to them instantly when you refresh. If you want to, you can basically have AP buffed diseases up ~70% of the fight.


Dunno if that was exactly the answer you're looking for. But I think it mostly covers it. The mobility fights I don't have an answer to - just minimize running in any way you can. Refresh diseases before said mob is gonna run off.

In essence: GoD is better than the three other glyphs by far. It's roughly twice as good as the second best glyph in every fight in ICC. You also get one extra global (SS instead of PS/IT) per one cycle, which is nice in almost every encounter. I'm very much GCD-starved in a lot of fights.
