I know I'm not from Paragon, but I reckon I can safely answer your questions.

The spreadsheet is 99.9% trustworthy, its calculations are very accurate and doing what it says is almost always the thing to do (unless its something stupid like 2 dps).

The white crit cap is not worth going for, you sacrifice too much dps in other stats. The poison hit cap is about 315, and with +5% hit from talents and raid buffs you can drop your hit considerably like the Paragon rogues have.
The spreadsheet will tell you to gem hit if its worth doing so.

I'm not sure about what macro you're talking about, but switching weapons is a significant dps loss. The only reason i can think for switching weapons is to apply crippling poison on val'kyr for LK or to put wound poison on saurfang, although neither of these things are necessary.

I hope that helped to answer your questions.

There's one important thing you need to remember with spreadsheets, they calculate theoretical dps in a standstill fight. There's pretty much only two of those in icc, saurfang and festergut. Some items (mainly herkuml) with procs have huge disadvantages in fights where the proc gets wasted or the buff drops because of moving or phase changes and the spreadsheet doesn't take this into account.

Got no idea about the macro you're talking about, the only weapon switch macro I have is for a spare offhand in case I need to remove some enrages on lk when our hunters haven't shown up.

236 or 237 (can't remember which, doesn't really matter) hit will be enough raid buffed, in bis gear levels arp and expertise are both more valuable than hit, so there's really no reason to have more unless you're already capped on arp and expertise or get socket bonuses by doing so.
