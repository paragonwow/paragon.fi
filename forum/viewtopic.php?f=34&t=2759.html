We have warlocks on knockback duty, with some help from shadow priests. Ranged is better for knockbacks in phase 4, well because they are ranged and have more spells to save the day with. Warlocks also take a bit less damage, as well a shadow priests, but get knockbacks to their spells. Hunters are an option as well, but they are quite squishy.

We have 2 people assigned on each meteor, the main knockbacker and a backup. The backup takes over when the main knocker kites, or has too many stacks.

The meteor kiting is teamwork with the players who knock back the meteors and the ones who kite them. The ideal would be having the meteors circling the raid as far away as possible, and to accomplish that, kiting is needed. If you just knock back them as fast as it's possible (the debuff/buff drops off every 5 seconds), the meteors will slowly creep too close to your raid and blow you up.

For instance, if I get a meteor I instantly run backwards from my spot and take a look at where the meteor is coming from. Let's assume the meteor is directly to the north, and the raid is at the middle of the platform. So the meteor starts coming for me from north, so I decide to start running straight to the east. That way the meteor will instantly start moving away from the direction of the raid diagonally. I continue running east, and as the meteor gets closer to me I start going more to south east, and then finally directly to south side of the circle the platform forms. Now, if the raid is at the middle, they should have range to the meteor all the time. So when the knock backer sees a good spot, they just knock the meteor back from the "inside" of the circle the meteor kiter has been running. That way the meteor will always end up getting knocked back quite far from the raid.

Let's assume the first kiter managed to move the meteor to the east side of the platform and it gets knocked back from the middle to the very most eastern corner the platform has. The next guy getting the kite has the option to either run north, or south to keep the meteor as far away form the raid as possible.

This tactic works quite good also when the raid will be too far out for the knockers to help. Then the kiter can just run long as possible and get the meteor as far away as he can, and then do a knock back (making sure it won't be to the direction where the raid is at).

The most important thing is to start moving instantly when the kite comes, and keep the meteor on the sides of the platform. That way you won't need to panic that much with new kites.
