Well my first reaction, which I apologize, was "Holy crap" (exactly what I said) when I saw your haste. Every simcraft I run shows haste being pretty important and I try to run around 19-20% haste. The new firelands gear is not very forgiving when it comes to haste, hence Xeno running alot of t11 gear, rings, neck those sorts of things, well my guess of why she is running with those.

Mastery is also, on sim, very low priority. It have increased a little in the past patches but still not as important as haste. And you run around 15 which will definitely help your MB, with its 6.5 sec cd. Increase in damage with the high of cd is not quite as important until you reach 4 piece t12 which will make it even better.

Your reforging baffles me though. You take away haste on some stats while adding haste to remove another stat kinda seems redundant. You are correct in thinking around 15% hit, while I keep around ~14%, is fine. But you should never reforge out of haste. Keep all your haste as is and add more and more if you can, you want as many ticks of your dots as you can get. You have reforged out of 403 haste which makes me a sad panda. That would be something I would look into.

On a side not, you have glyph of PoM? in shadow spec. I would change that out for something useful while as a shadow priest. The one time you should be healing, if at all, is a divine hymn if your healing are in trouble and they call for it but other then that, no. Fade or dispel magic is common, and spirit tap.

Oh and the dps thing, I run into the same thing where I can run good and bad dps from fight to fight. So not to worry. As long as you keep up your empowered shadow, that is a biggy.

I'll first answer this one. Indeed, when you get haste buff, their effects are multiplicative and not additive. Though you final haste is calculated as follows :
haste = current haste on stuff * Dark Intent * Shaman Totem
Though for a basis of 20% haste you finally get :
haste = 1.20 * 1.03 * 1.05 = (1.236 ) * 1.05 = 1.2978
see here the dark intent gave you 3.6 % of total haste

On the other hand for your stat issue, having a relatively low hit value under cap it not really a concern as a SP because once your DoTs are applied on your target they're not subject to miss (i.e. DoTs ticks cannot miss ). I would suggest to maintain a level between 15% and hit cap to avoid big surprises.
For the stat priority then I would suggest you to have a look on haste caps. Those caps represents the values at which you get one more tick on some of your DoTs.I've not the numbers right here but I'm pretty sure you'll find them easily.

Then to make it simple :
If reforging / gemming haste allows you to reach one haste cap then : haste >> mast > crit
Otherwise, I would say : mastery >= haste > crit
