Spine of Deathwing [25] Heroic:
Any suggestions for this dreaded boss? We have been currently able to master the 1st Tendon, though when we reach the 2nd Tendon, the beginning is challenging. We've been trying to flush all the bloods before starting the 2nd tendon damage, so we misdirection/multi-shot the bloods to the tank, and then stack to flip in the right corner. After that we continue with DPSing the Amalg, and breaking the Tendon.

Items:
Now we are aware that valor trinkets are extremely powerful on this fight, and everyone is required to wear one. We also know that Gurthalak's are also not the greatest for this fight as well. Would it be better for hunters to use their Kiril, Fury of Beasts?

Hunter Questions:
I am currently main speccing a Survival Hunter, and have been trying to figure out the best things that could be done on this fight, though I still have a few questions. Is the Raptor Strike Glyph useful for this encounter? I've been told that it would be better suited for Deterence. Secondly, I've noticed before that Devai was using a turtle. Is the use of Intervene from turtle-like pets worth it in this fight? Or is that only good for fights such as Madness? I've also been told that Marksmanship is the greater spec on this fight, - Any word for this?

Fiery Grip:
We don't have much trouble with Fiery Grip, but a question has come to us recently. We normally have each and every range turn around and shoot 1 spell at it, while a hunter will Arcane Shot it multiple times. Would it be easier to just assign the 2 hunters on the fiery grip, and Arcane Shot 3-6 times each?

Any other suggestions:
Is there anything you have in mind that we could do different, or anything we should try?



Thank you,

I tank this fight, and we haven't downed it quite yet, so take my advice on this one matter for what it is.

I read one hunter's post and he said that he switched in Kiril about 45-60 seconds before the tendon popped. This allowed the staff enough time to get its stacks up, while ensuring it would not be on CD while the tendon was up. (you may want to check the timing on that, I think that was it though)
