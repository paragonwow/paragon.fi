Vongol,

There was a recent post about this exact issue so please refer to that for more details.

Your logic in the pattern of spreading combustion is where I believe the problem may be. You do not want to do anything that will disrupt ignite or cause it fall of when you are approaching that short 5-10 second golden window for spreading combustion. If you get a hot streak proc before you enter the window save it. You want to fire it off when there is a fresh ignite on the target that has not ticked yet. Then either the fireball you cast after that ignite went on the target will crit or the pyroblast. If neither crit that is simply bad rng you have to deal with it and spread combustion anyway. At this point if you already have the proc to spread combustion use it as soon as it appears on the boss. If not use blats wave after applying combustion and the spread the dot. Since you would have to wait a second for combustion to register anyway, there isn't much of a loss here. All of the adds will be stack up so the chance of 0 out of nearly 60 opportunities for Impact to proc is minuscule, however, if it does not proc use Flame Strike until you do get a proc and then spread combustion.

Gothen,

Dragonwrath procs have the ability to crit even if the first one did not. Simply enough this crit will factor into ignite. It does not create a new dot on the target. It will increase the stregth of the one currently applied. If both Dragonwrath's proc and the spell you cast crit only one will be factored into ignite and the other will be menched because two crits that hit at the exact same time will not cause ignite to stack.

Simply answer: Does it make ignite bigger? It makes bigger ignite more frequent.


Hope it helps,

-Guromin
