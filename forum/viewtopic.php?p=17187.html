Looks like an interesting project and hope it goes well for you!

I would just add that it's important to keep in mind that you may not want to have too strong a conclusion as to why people select particular roles. Sometimes a role is thrust on them or sometimes the raid leader ends up being the tank more because it's a fundamental (but far more finite) role that needs steady attendance and availability not because they necessarily "want" to be the tank--you're as likely to find raid leaders doing a job because they know it needs doing and not only because they like it.

I would also say that if you want to look at more than one type of raiding guild across the spectrum of raiding (though I'd suggest that's quite complex for a short term project like this) you have to consider the different motivating factors behind role selection. A raider in a more competitively minded guild is less likely to retain strong attachment to a particular type of class or role, for example, than a raider in a far more casually oriented guild. The needs and outcomes of a guild often help dictate role preference or assignment.
