Personally...I avoid to use pure 40 mastery gems simply because of the base effect of death strike (minimum 7% of my total health) My healers generally tell me that I'm a lot easier to heal because I simply time my death strikes well enough to get maximum healing + absorb. By doing this i keep bone shield on me 99% of a fight. This means that during the entire fight I'm taking only 80% damage without even popping real cooldowns. For HC Alysrazor, the part you die is probably the hatchlings? Once the hungry status comes back on I simply tank the hatching literally at the edge of the bird needing to eat. Once enraged I pop Fortitude or Rune Weapon + Vamp Blood and I've never actually died. Also ask your healers to watch for the bleeding debuff that disappears at 50% health it's simply much harder to keep the tank alive with that debuff on. The only other time i think you might die is then the boss lands and recovers from being dazed. At this point since I've used fortitude, I only have fortitude on cooldown so I have other cooldowns to pop. I always summon ghoul here just in case and pop vam blood to cover for me tanking the first time and rune weapon on the second tank. Then AMS when the boss flies up again. I sure hope this helps you.
Also gear problems. I still really recommend 4% parry over stone gargoyle. I'm not sure but it's always worked out better for me. Also, my threat has never been a problem so I gave up on blood cake and went for Abomination's Might because 2% extra strength means extra dodge rating. I don't actually have combat data from you but I strongly recommend getting right of primary glyph death strike and use rune strike. 1. It provides more threat 2. You should be using rune strike for all your runic power 3. You should have enough runic power at any given point to make the extra couple % of damage mean anything. Remember more rune strike = more death strikes.

Aßaddon wrote:Ty for the advice m8 il try it out tonight , but tbh im not having problems with the bird aswell , its just that healers said i take alot of dmg .

This is just the nature of DK tanks, unfortunately. DKs take more damage than other tanks, but they heal it back.

The drawback is that if bosses hit hard enough to kill you before you can Death Strike, which happens a lot in 25-man heroics, then you'll be a lot squishier than other tanks.

There's actually a lot of discontent about this in the tanking community right now, because DKs have noticeable survivability problems in 25-man heroic raids. Check out the WoW tanking forums sometime, and you'll see what I mean.
