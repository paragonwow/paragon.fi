The "information" here is amazing, so let's set a few things straight:

Free disk space makes absolutely no difference whatsoever in your framerates. It is a good idea to have some free space, but a few GB is plenty enough to avoid any real trouble. (Sidenote: With SSD drives this becomes slightly more complex and you do in fact want to keep more free space around to make wear leveling more efficient. But even that is not something that affects your framerates in games.)

While changing the process priority might help if you have a ton of background tasks running, it's generally a better idea to shut down those background tasks in the first place instead.

If you have "monitors" inside your computer, you might want to hit the person who built it with the nearest heavy blunt object. Cleaning dust from the fans and heatsinks is a good idea though - modern graphics cards and CPUs throttle down to lower speed if they overheat.



Now, with all the weird stuff out of the way, let's see what we can actually do to improve things. Some easy stuff to begin with:

First, remove all addons you can live without. Seriously. With a CPU that slow you really can't afford any extra stuff, and that means you run with only a bossmod and maybe Grid or somesuch if you're a healer. No dps meters, fancy chat mods or anything else that is not absolutely essential for being able to do your job. If you don't raid you might want to go completely without addons.

Second, exit all other programs while you play. Even if they're not using a whole lot of CPU they still use memory.

Third, check that you have reasonably recent graphics drivers - they do improve those every now and then, after all.



If those don't really improve things, you can try the macros from this thread. Ignore the "m2faster" stuff, that should be already fixed in the game itself I believe. Just try the 3 first macros and see if they help. I'm not sure if they still work with the most recent version of the game but I tested them on a very slow laptop a while ago and they really made a difference. The game becomes rather ugly though...



For advanced users who know what they're doing (DISCLAIMER: Not my fault if you mess up your computer!):

Stop and disable all unnecessary services, Windows has quite a lot of useless stuff running by default.

Overclock your CPU and graphics card.



Looking at the computer specs you posted I'd say you simply can't get very good performance, especially in Dalaran during the rush hour. The game simply isn't optimized for displaying a lot of player models in one area, especially on low end computers.
