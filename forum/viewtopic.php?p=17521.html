What is cool about dk is that they can generate a certain amount of self-healing, but if the damage they receive starts going over what they can self heal, they will become weaker than warrior and Paladins as the amount of damage they receive grows. Take for example the bloods on heroic spine of deathwing. Sure the dk could make it trought a few with his self heal, but because he doesnt have block, he could be obliterated faster by 100 bloods than a paladin would be (block). Thats why they make a good tank for the amalgamation, because they require very little heal untill you bring it to over 3 stacks.

And paladins or warrior would be a better choice for tanking/kiting a big amount of blood.

Druid has a versatibility of being able to turn to cat when not tanking (Example, waiting for debuffs to wear out on any two tank boss fights (to do more dps ofcourse)). They also have a certain amount of absorbs like dks so they can keep themselves alive in some situations. Again, it could be a weaker choice against a great amount of damage incoming (less passive mitigation (no block)).

Just to say, all class are equivalent overall, but have their weakness and strenght that can be beneficial or dangerous in many situations.

Ofcourse, no content is uncompletable with a certain tank, but it can greatly help to chose the right tanks for every situation. Something high end raiding guilds can certainly afford.
