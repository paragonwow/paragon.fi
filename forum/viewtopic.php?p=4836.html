The latest big change was the complete overhaul and revamp of the talent point system and the way players get to use it. The simple question then is: What do you think about it?

I personally like the changes a lot because I think it gives WoW some more simplicity without making it boring. Over time Blizzard has made a lot of changes to the way players level (More XP rewarded, less XP for a level, Heirlooms, RAF) but this one feels the best so far for me. You get to stuck with one tree for a long time, helping new players to now completely fuck up their char by spending points in all trees (like I did when I started). For experienced players, allthough a lot of the talents are similar, this will still make leveling a little different and interesting maybe. Just to experience the alternation between points and abilities that one receives on a level up.

Combined with the LFG system it should lead to better groups on low levels, even if people do not have Heirlooms / PvP items. The role of a char is clear, and there are less non-sense abilities around.

The revamp has tickled me into trying to level a new char in Beta and once Cata is out just to experience this whole system from the start. Job well done imo Blizzard.

I'm not entirely sure what arx is talking about - the trees probably just aren't quite as interesting with druid as they are with some other classes. If they aren't, I'm hoping the less interesting trees and specs get some love and actually have some real choices to make. I'd like it if there were some more situational talents added still that wouldn't benefit your character 24/7.

Overall, I like the change a lot. There was such a huge amount of dead weight hanging on the talent trees. I'm slightly impressed that the development team can make such a radical design decision at this point still. A lot of the passive talents were just very boring and mandatory anyway - and you had to include similar ones for a lot of classes.

Only gripe I got is that I feel like we're still lacking ~5 talent points even at max level. There would be a ton more interesting specs to use and choices to do if there were a few more points and few more active skills and situational talents. For example, choosing between filling up the last lucrative talents in main tree that are on the bottom which give a clear damage bonus vs. grabbing a cooldown skill in another tree.
