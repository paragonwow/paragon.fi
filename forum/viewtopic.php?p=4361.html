Instant poison is PPM, so it has a higher chance of proccing (per hit) the slower your weapon speed is. You should be aware of which weapon you put the poison on, not which hand. Deadly benefits from a fast weapon because it has a static % chance of proccing. Thus instant naturally goes on the slower one.

After you get your poisons the right way around you can start thinking about which of the weapons has higher dps. Higher dps one always goes to your main hand and if you happen to have 2 similar dps weapons it's better to have the faster one in your MH. In case both of your weapons are of similar speed and dps the one with deadly poison should be in your main hand. This of course applies only to mutilate.

Even if you have 8% (or whatever) worth of hit rating your main hand will be able to miss. As long as you have a weapon in your offhand your white attacks will have the 27%(I think it's 27, doesn't really matter) chance of missing before you factor in any talents or hit rating. Special attacks have a 8% chance of missing, most of that is already attainable from combat talents so basically you need only 3% of hit rating to make yellow attacks hit 100%. You should however aim to keep your hit rating at the poison hit cap, which is 236 with all raid buffs and 5/5 precision.

I think the current assassination in the alpha will see an overhaul in the upcoming builds. Judging from combat and the statements that blizzard has given they want the trees to have spots where you need to pick utility talents to move forward and right now the alpha-assassination is pretty bloated with dps talents and the same goes for subtlety.

The thing I'm kinda bummed about is the variety of filler talents in combat. Everything except endurance and imp sprint seem useless in pve and I'd like to see some of them changed to give a bit more pve utility. When you compare stuff like Imp Gouge, Deflection, Reinforced Leather, Unfair Advantage, Throwing Spec, Imp Kick and both of the recuperate talents to say Fleet Footed, Quick Recovery, Imp Expose, Deadly Brew, Dirty Tricks, Elusiveness, Enveloping Shadows or Cheat Death you can see the clear difference in the quality of the talents.

The mastery system is there to help balance the trees, even though there might be differences initially blizzard can always tweak the values of mastery bonuses to make the specs about even. This might cause us to not see such huge changes as rupture having crit built in or the recent deadly poison change, but atleast it's an easy way to keep things decently balanced. Personally I think we will be fine as they're giving us more of the same we have been given until now and it has worked fine for quite a while. Would be nice to see something new for a change though. For example that combo point self-stacking is long overdue and I still don't think redirect is going to cut it with 1 min cd.
