Edit: Also, why is 17/0/54 (or some variation) the best spec for arthas? I dont find myself using DnD very much on the fight, however I do use GoD for it.

The part of the fight where you really, really want to use DnD on is at the Val'kyr phase. I even glyph it instead of ghoul on that fight. There's really nothing more important than getting them down. As long as the damage isn't overkill, it's very good DPS. I usually get to cast 1-2 of them on the heroic mode, hitting 3 Val'kyrs plus LK at the same time.

Now, depending on the raid DPS, it might not be worth glyphing it just for normal mode. If that part of the fight is tight, though, there's really no reason not to glyph for it. Boosting the rDPS / helping out the raid is far more important than personal DPS.

9k does sound a bit low, considering you have the +5% buff up. Is this 10-man or 25-man? Are you absolutely sure you had sunders/expose up in that raid? You know already that you're missing expertise, but you're also a bit over the hit required for alliance (assuming you have some draenei in your raid). Get the pants (or some other +expertise items) asap - it stings really bad when you get dodged when using GoD. You're also missing +5% melee crit in the setup you described.

ArP isn't a bad stat by any means. I don't mind picking up items with ArP on them, but I'd never gem for it as UH or in any way prioritize them. If it's an overall better piece than another one in the same slot, I'll definitely use it. However, by far majority of the fights have multiple targets. ArP doesn't do jack shit compared to strength (and even crit) in such fights.

On single target, I suspect strength might be losing out at stupid high levels of ArP, but I haven't bothered to do any math on it. This is simply because there's one encounter left for me in the game to kill that I haven't done and +str gemming is far superior in it as well. Even so, I wouldn't bother gemming for ArP unless it was roughly twice as good as STR.
