I've also been noticing the same thing that others have commented on - from the findings so far, it seems like the more common personality types among raiders are actually some of the rarest ones in the original study. And there especially seems to be fewer E's and F's - the lower frequency of E's could have to do with the physical nature of playing MMO's, namely mainly being physically removed from eachother (ofc there are also some that play in internet cafe's). And the lower frequency of F's might have to do with the often demanding and very direct nature of raiding, at least progress focused raiding at higher levels - it being competative, often with direct objective feedback and high stress levels. Maybe this setting is "harder to love" for the F's? Then again we also know how much fun and social interaction we have, but maybe some of these other factors already contribute to fewer of the E's and F's starting to do raiding altogether? And finally it seems like there is a low amount of females posting their results - does this percentage correspond to the expected percentage of females in raiding guilds? And if not, why?
