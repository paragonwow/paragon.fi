Jhazrun
 
Posts: 390
In a vacuum, 'fine' is not a poor choice at all to describe a 90%+ effectiveness rate for one's HPS…

That, however, is what Discipline is based on for just about each and every boss out there, and this time it finds itself in an encounter balanced around the others approaching true healing with two-to-three times its maximum output.

To make things worse, you're not actually "shielding" anyone against anything! Any kind of pre-emptive spike buffering is impossible due to being limited to a few active half-powered shields at a time.
The very most blindly thrown-around PW:Ss wind up doing is lose all of their potency over the course of a couple of damage aura ticks that would've been taken care of in no time at all by semi-automatic raid heals anyway.

Put in general form, no one in these kinds of encounters dies to anything Discipline would have a reasonable chance to counter. This's a direct result of the passive damage reducing the effective duration of any given PW:S to no more than a handful of seconds, leaving you futilely attempting to contribute with neither the HPS of a raid healer nor your innate utility.
