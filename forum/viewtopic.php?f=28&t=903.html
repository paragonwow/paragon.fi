Ah, the classic choice between what's fun and what does the best dps. The choice at the level I play is rather obvious, your choice is, of course, your own.

Sosul wrote:I was playing around with Rawr and it said those two pieces would increase my dps by around 200 dps. Of course, Rawr was also the program that suggested I use agi/stam gems.


I'd recommend taking the suggestions of Rawr with a large grain of salt - the hunter simulation is not very accurate. I'd recommend either the spreadsheet (or the online version of it at http://www.femaledwarf.com/ which is very easy to use) or simulationcraft.

Sosul wrote:My hit is so high because I just don't have the right choices for the gear that I want. Hit is a lower priority for me right now, but if it's the difference between a 264 piece and a 277 piece, the other stats make up for the choice. However, since switching those pieces, I'm below hit now. I usually have a dranei in my party, but if it becomes a big deal I can just do a race change, it's not a big deal.


Hit should really be the highest priority. As in, optimizing it to be as close to 8% as possible, but not over. As MM you can always swap points in and out of Focused Aim which makes optimizing hit easier. If you can't get exactly 8% it's better to be slightly over than slightly under, since just one missed Chimera Shot is huge dps loss.

Some very rough stat values, from memory since I'm too lazy to rerun the numbers:
1 hit rating = 5dps
1 arpen rating = 2.8dps
1 agi = 2.5dps
1 crit rating = 1.5dps
1 haste rating = 0.8dps
1 int = 0.7dps

DISCLAIMER: The numbers are probably way off, but the relative scales should be in the rough ballpark.

Note that some of the statvalues scale very nonlinearly, namely arpen is not very good until you get 1000+, after which every point is worth more and more until you hit the hardcap at 1400.



I notice you're now MM - there are a few things I'd like to note about that spec. First of all, 3/3 Focused Aim is unnecessary with the amount of hit you have from gear, only take enough points to get over 8% hit total. Secondly, you really shouldn't skip Improved Steady Shot completely for Improved Hunter's Mark, not when you need points in Focused Aim also. It's probably better to drop points from Improved Barrage than Improved Steady Shot anyway.

I'd recommend something like http://www.wowhead.com/talent#cxbZMeVoRhzGIctuAox00b for your spec - put the last point in TSA if no one else brings the buff in your raid, otherwise I'd put it in Rapid Recuperation. IHM makes sense if you run with 3+ hunters in raid, but then one of the hunters should gear with enough hit to not need any points in Focused Aim so he can take IHM without losing any important talents.

Also note that as MM you're somewhat more dependent on others putting their debuffs up - without sunders/expose and a bleed debuff you won't be doing much dps at all as MM.
