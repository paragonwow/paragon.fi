@Opex

Obviously you’re entitled to your opinions, but I have to say I disagree with you quite a lot. Firstly, I find X-Perl to be a great UI-addon which is configurable and clear. Probably the only thing I really need from my unit frames is that I can see my dot timers on my target, and X-Perl accomplishes that just fine.

Secondly, about those dot trackers (or as you probably also meant, cooldown trackers). On single target fights, my main dot tracker is the one on my target, X-Perl dot tracker. On multi target fights I can see my dots from TidyPlates as well. The dot timers above my cast bar aren’t actually supposed to even be there, I usually configure them away as I don’t need them there. Then about those cooldown trackers, I have them in a few places, yes, but only because I have categorized them according to their nature (e. g. on the right side are buffs that should always be up like Empowered Shadows, on the left side are actual cooldown buffs like Volcanic Potion). With Power Auras I follow the most important cd’s and buffs, like Shadow Orbs and Shadowfiend.

And thirdly, your comment about my un-girly UI.. In my opinion my UI is quite neat and simple which brings with it (I believe) efficiency. The UI might not be the prettiest or filled with butterflies etc. but I can assure you that during beta testing and progressing it’s not the wisest thing to have 100+1 addons. Half of them aren’t properly updated at the time which means you can’t be blindly depending on them – having skills in using default UI can come in handy then.

Every player is used to a certain type of UI and people have different opinions in what is efficient and beautiful. The fact that my UI works for me doesn’t mean that it should work for everyone else.
