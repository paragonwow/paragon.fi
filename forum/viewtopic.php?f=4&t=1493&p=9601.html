I'll just drop in a comment here since I have a bit different view on this than others who have posted.

I mostly didn't enjoy playing my alt mainly because for this expansion it wasn't a healer. I mostly don't enjoy playing offspecs. While the occasional romp with balance, prot or arcane may be fun it's not really something I see myself enjoying regularly. There's a pretty narrow band of what I enjoy in this game and that's trying to perfect some aspects of my gameplay after what usually requires hundreds of hours of practice. Some people will be faster learners and some will enjoy themselves even before they feel they've mastered their role, that's just plain not me, though.

Then again, my whole attitude to the expansion was quite defeatist from the start (knowing that resto druids were unlike to earn raid spots with any consistency during the first tier). 6 years ago I initially gravitated towards healy druid because it felt like a solid option (for those raid spots). For Cataclysm I had hoped to level a priest instead of druid for that express purpose but that sorta fell through and instead I ended up with a mage for alt (which I didn't quite enjoy plus there was plenty of mages anyway). The end result is that I used the mage for archaeology (at least I got some shiny epics out of it). :)

Still, I think it's a shame how the lack of that final polish has greatly diminished from the enjoyment of this tier of raiding for many people. These things could (should) have been fixed if not for the rush to get the game out before Christmas. On the whole it's probably still the best tier thus far.
