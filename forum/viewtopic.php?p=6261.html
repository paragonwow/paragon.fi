Hello Metrius.

I'm already sorry I can't really say much more than has already been said, because that's mostly what there is to it. There doesn't seem to be anything wrong with how you play from what I could tell from a quick glance at your logs. I do like how you described what you do as well, it saves me from trying to cover any of that and you from getting a reply you don't need. And no, from that part there doesn't seem to be much wrong about what you described either, although I'm not sure if I'm gonna buy you not running out of mana on 4-minute fights while not using too much LT.

As teh already mentioned, for example Bloodlust uptime plays a notable role when comparing your DPS with top-guild player DPS. Say if we kill Saurfang in about 2 minutes, it means we're getting about ((0.3x40):120)x100%=~10% average haste from Bloodlust for then entire fight while on your almost 4-minute kill it would be around ((0.3x40):240)x100%=~5%. And as one might surmise, without taking cooldowns and potions into account, on a kill twice as short you get twice the benefit from Bloodlust or Infernal.

And of course as has already been said, gear does in fact play a notable role, especially considering how stat values tend to scale upwards as you obtain more of them.

I can understand that obtaining better gear or running shorter fights is pretty hard if you run a 6/12 normal pug. However, if you really want to see if you can blast higher numbers with what you've got, I'd recommend doing two things. Firstly, using an Infernal at the right time will give you a DPS boost, and secondly, getting a Nevermelting Ice Crystal and using it whenever it's viable should get you even higher, especially if you keep up with Wild Magic pre-potions and strike a deal with a rogue for (preferably glyphed) early tricks that won't get you killed.

Lastly, having a good connection is always great, but if your framerates are low, the problem might just be in hardware.
Manni

The right time to pop Inferno is when the boss has 60 seconds left to live (or well, you should start casting a bit before that.) Addons like TimeToDie can help you estimate it. Wherever NMIC is viable, I'd use it over Muradin's, and I'd recommend speccing Dark Pact if it doesn't feel like you don't want another button for the same purpose.

I visited maxdps once but to me it seemed rather outdated and inaccurate so I didn't bother doing it again. If you're looking for up to date theorycrafting, I'd suggest you to spend some time in elitistjerks and with the latest spreadsheet, Rawr and/or simulationcraft.
