Hey,

All raiding shadow priests (and warlocks and boomkins=dot classes) always roll dots on multiple targets when possible. Usually there are 2 targets, and in that case it's enough that you have for instance your focus target's debuffs visible. If there are more than 2 it's all about tabbing through the targets and mind flaying and refreshing vt and throwing in MB's on good spots. My addons don't tell me the timers for more than 2 targets. To track my dots, I earlier used classtimer but now I have mfclip (which gives you tons of information about your overall performance).

Mana might be a proble with lower gear levels. I noticed that being under the hit cap affects your mana situation and makes it a bit more problematic, so I think you should try to aim for the cap if you have troubles. Also, make sure you use all the cooldowns that give you mana (DA and shadowfiend) on every cooldown. When moving don't spam DP but rather use SWD to gain mana. Also, I've been having serious mana problems on Cho'gall for instance, and if I see myself running low (around 15%) I ask for an innervate. Usually I get it, but if I don't, I'll just have to suck it and either use dispersion or keep deathing a bit more till my cooldowns come back. Going completely oom is the worst thing that can happen. Gear of course helps with this but doesn't remove completely let you play without looking at your manabar.

Halvan wrote:Ok, so basically more awareness with tabbing through targets when I multi-dot and maybe not blow all mana giving cooldowns to just increase dps but rather separately use them when mana is tight and roll them so I'm never without one right?


This is kind of tricky. We tend to use our bloodlust in the beginning of the fight when your weapon enchant and trinkets proc and you should use all your cooldowns with pre pot. It's a massive dps loss if you hold back your shadowfiend or DA at that point, so don't do that. Later just make sure you summon your fiend in a situation where you can make sure it gets all it's hits through and doesn't need to move too much.

Most of the fights have aoe phases and then single target phases (Maloriak for instance), so when you are in a single target phase you can use the situation to get mana through death and other cooldowns. On Maloriak I blow all my cooldowns in the beginning and I'm nearly oom when the black phase ends. Then I do single target dps untill green phase and while that my mana has regended near full. I blow a big portion of my mana on the adds in green phase. DA and fiend come off from cooldown before black phase so I use them there. After that I save them until we use bloodlust in the second green phase. It's all about knowing the fights and your mana situation :)
