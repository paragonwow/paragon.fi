Perhaps 'useless' might have been a bit too strong of word, perhaps a more accurate description of what he was saying would be 'top 3 weakest' classes.

Like Lazei said, for 99% of guilds you can pretty much use any class in your raid and if you're competent enough easily clear everything given the time. While DK/Hunter/Shaman may not be the BEST classes in the world in 4.3, they're still competitive enough to have a spot in everything but the elite of the elite of raiding.

However; in Paragon's (and a few other guilds) case they have the dedication and resources for their members to play any class that they need. This, along with the fact that they have 12 (by my count) legendary staves, makes certain classes indeed 'useless' for them. With Paragon's ability to have a full raid with nearly ANY class-combination, why on earth would they not go for the absolute optimal set-up? Why bring a DK when they could have Warriors that do more DPS or Rogues that are working on Legendaries and do more DPS? Why bring a Hunter when you could have a caster with a Legendary that does more? Why bring a Resto Shaman when a Paladin/Priest/Druid does more healing in the situations they're in? Where in every case the player behind the class is playing to near perfection.

So while the differences between classes may appear slight (and may indeed be slight)to most guilds, for Paragon it may make all the difference between World 1st and World 2nd.

But who knows! Perhaps Paragon will find some crazy strategy involving DKs or Hunters that gets them the World 1st Heroic Madness of Deathwing kill!

TL;DR: This only applies to guilds like Paragon, do whatever you want with these classes unless your going for World 1st and everyone in your raid plays near-perfect.
