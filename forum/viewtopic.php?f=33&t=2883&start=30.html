Hailofthechamber,

As a general rule I would have either your t12 or t13 4 set active and complete. I would not attempt to use the t12 2 set and the t13 2 set simultaneously because the benefit from them is minuscule in comparison to either 4 set. Simulationcraft will tell you that using the LFR t13 4 set is a dps loss in comparison to the 391 4 set, but I would acquire those items anyway. Calculating which is better is not easy by any means. I, personally, side with using the t13 4 set even it is shown to be a dps loss on Simulation Craft. Here's why:

  • Any Fight where it can be spread makes it a significant dps gain.
  • The duration of a fight can also impact the value of the set bonus
  • I'm skeptical that Simulation Craft does not account for Ignite stacking

Hopefully this clears things up about the tier set. For those who wish to rely on only numbers, the set bonus becomes an increase when 3 items are 397 and only one is 384.

OniHouse,

Lightning Rod was considered for BiS, however, the increased item level, haste proc, and buff to other players make Ti'tahk, the Steps of Time better. Anyone playing a guild that does not normally clear the content quickly and does not have Dragonwrath should consider rolling on Lightning Rod because chances are the next upgrade is a ways down the road.

-Guromin

Guromin

Kadesha,

I don't really have a good answer for you. Theoretically, with terrible luck, it's possible to never crit once in an entire fight. While this is unlikely, it is still theoretically possible. On a more practical note, there are a few things that can persuade me to wait or get Combustion on CD. I try to be aware of all my buffs, the duration of the fight, and the "Golden Window" (as I like to call it).

Predicting when your spells will crit is difficult, but the probability is increased when all buffs are up. There are few instances (with the t13 4 set) that I turn down a 20k ignite and wait for something better. The first of which is at the start of a fight. If I can get a 20k ignite within the first 5 seconds, chances are Something a little higher will come about. If nothing does within 10 seconds I'll take the next 20k ignite.

I do want to mention why I use the value 20k. My Pyrobast! crits are capable of producing ignites of 20k without any additional buffs (trinkets and intellect procs), encounter damage buffs, or ignite stacking. Determining 20k, was based on observation instead of math because the actual number serves as a guideline instead of an exact measurement.

So, Kadesha, I don't really know what else I can tell you other than your going to win some by waiting and your going to loose some. Just know the goal, and your surroundings to increase the number the times you win.

-Guromin
