You should search EJ for some answers, but I guess I could do it for you just this once.

The ep values for trinkets can be found in this post.
It lists prestor slightly above tia's grace but what it does not take into account is the fight specific factors like burst phases, movement or the duration of the fight. Proc trinkets gain decent advantages over static stacking trinkets when they proc during bloodlust or other cooldowns. They often also provide the same amount of stats than the stacking trinkets on average which means that there's a good chance that the fight ends at a point in time where the proc trinket is on cooldown and isn't about to proc again in a while thus providing a greater benefit than the static trinket.

For trinkets in general, Yurifei posted a trinket chart on the elitits jerks forums a while back. It and Aldriana's analysis on the chart can be found here. It's probably not 100% accurate, but a good outline.

As you can see, Key to the Endless Chamber exceeds all trinkets except heroic prestor's during the first 2 minutes and continues to steadily descend from there. Only at around 7 or so minutes do EoC372 and prestor 359 start to equalize with the key. This chart also ignores all other factors except time, which in reality amplifies the key proc even more. Think about fights like al'akir, maloriak, chimaeron or ascendant council. All of those have some kind of a burst phase where you need huge damage and other phases where your damage doesn't really matter all that much. Having a proc that gives 1710 agility for 15 seconds is just huge when compared to 2178 crit for 10s in those situations. I, for example, have the EoC372, but I'd rather wear the key instead and have damage where it matters in exchange for a slightly worse overall dps. I feel that the devs kinda failed the agility trinket design when they decided not to have any of them actually proc agility and gave us something gimmicky like unheeded warning which ended up being worse than all blue trinkets for all classes.
