Badkarma wrote:Also had one more question regarding lich king. We just started a few heroic attempts this week (only like 5) and will be casually doing progress on him. I feel most of it is very straightforward DPS wise except for DPSing the Valkyr's as a hunter. I have to run into the stack, sit there for a bit when they start coming until I find they don't pick me, then get back out like 10 yards due to their big hit boxes. Is there a more efficient method to doing DPS on them than this as a hunter? Do you know if feigning when they are about to pick targets take you off the list to be picked up / would it be worth it if so?


There's a few things about Val'kyrs and hunters. First of all, it is possible to use Disengage to get back to the platform after being dropped outside, but the timing is quite hard (in fact I wouldn't want to try relying on it without Levitate, and that's unreliable because there's damage from Infest etc) so since the dps requirement isn't totally insane you can just collapse to melee.

However, instead of just collapsing early when the timer runs out, what you do is this:

a) stand just outside the group, outside deadzone
b) have BigWigs
c) as soon as BigWigs says "Val'kyr" in the middle of your screen (better have that message enabled!) you run into the pile
d) once in the pile, drop Explosive Trap and start doing Volley until the third Val'kyr has landed, then move out (use Disengage if you think you won't need it for anything else)

Obviously you still won't do as much damage as the others can do but at least this way you're never really be completely idle. Also if I'm very low on mana I switch to Aspect of the Viper just before the Val'kyrs grab people - so if I get grabbed I'll be regening mana during the time I can't do anything anyway.

Feigning out of the threatlist probably doesn't work, although I haven't done complete testing on the matter, you'd still lose a lot of dps time sitting feigned until the Val'kyrs have chosen their targets so it doesn't seem very viable.

Deepfriedegg wrote:Kruf, I think you forgot about Piercing Shots talent, which deals 30% (39% with debuff on target) additional damage over 8 seconds of Chimera, Aimed and Steady shot, if that shot have critted. This talent doesnt affect the Serpent portion of chimera so in fact you would need more ticks - on average 2 or 3. Exact number depends on: 1) Has Steady Shot critted (very likely it had) 2) How many times Piercing Shots will tick before the target dies. 3) How many times will Serpent tick before target dies

This leaves us with so much variables that before you would find out what shot to use, you would have lost your GCD anyway :) To be honest, I would go with Steady.


Right you are, let's see some tweaked math:

One full Piercing Shots from a Steady Shot crit does about 2.4k damage over 8sec, ticking every second, so about 300/tick.

In most parses I have roughly 62-63% crit rate on Steady, so we can calculate that "on average" the Piercing from the first Steady does 0.63*2400 = 1512 damage over 8sec.

So, for 8sec case we have:

Code: Select all
5922 Steady
1512 Piercing ticks
=
7434 total


vs

Code: Select all
5320 Chimera - Serpent
987 Serpent tick
987 Serpent tick
=
7294 total


So, in case the mob dies after 8sec but before 9sec, you'll have done slightly more damage on average without the sting, however the difference is very small and the damage is much less "steady" depending more on your crits. Note also that casting Steady Shot takes 0.2sec more time unless some haste buff is up, further closing the gap.
