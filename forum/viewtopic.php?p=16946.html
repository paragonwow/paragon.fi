Cooleled,

Combustion combines all the period damaging abilities you've placed on the target (Ignite, Pyroblast, living Bomb, and Frostfire Bolt if you're using Frostfire..which i highly recommend not doing.) These will take the current strength of all the damge over time abilities you've placed on the target and duplicate them and lable this new very large DoT "Combustion"

So, this means it's best to use Combustion when you have high periodic damage on your target. The best way to achieve this is to ensure you have all your periodic damage abilities on the target. Your biggest source of periodic damage for combustion is going to be your ignite. To get a large ignite you need a heavy hitting Fireball or Pyroblast crit. This is best achieved through intellect/spell power procs or increased damage periods. i.e.; Hagara after storm/ice phases or on Deathwing during Cataclysm.

But basically if i lost you in all that..

Get a large ignite along with a living bomb and pyroblast DoT.

Geezup,

You said you want to be in the upper 30's, but that's entirely dependent on the fight. I will assume you mean Ultraxion in preparation for heroic.

First of all check out my Ignite guide if you haven't already. It will help you to do more combustion damage.

Right now you are using Ti'Thak, which will do better raid damage, but lower personal damage. Not a problem, but something to keep in mind. Make sure to be aware of when it procs. During this time you will want to avoid using spells like Flame Orb that are unaffected by haste. If your trinket and staff are up at the same time you will want to be even more conscious of what spells you cast. Using Combustion while both are up will result in significantly more damage, so be willing to take a little smaller ignite while these are up (especially if there is any AoE).

Take advantage of dot spreading. I don't know how you do Gunship, but we tank the adds on top of the right drake, which allows for some amazing Combustion damage. This is even more important in the heroic version.

Your reforging has very little crit on it, which I strongly advice against. Here is a more optimal reforging set up:

Neck : MasteryRating -> CritRating
Shoulders : MasteryRating -> HitRating
Chest : HitRating -> CritRating
Waist : MasteryRating -> HitRating
Legs : MasteryRating -> HitRating
Wrists : MasteryRating -> HitRating
Hands : MasteryRating -> CritRating
Ring1 : HasteRating -> CritRating
Ring2 : HitRating -> CritRating
Back : MasteryRating -> CritRating
Ranged : MasteryRating -> CritRating

I also noticed your Living Bomb uptime is not very good. I would work on refreshing that faster because you are losing dps every second it is not on the target. Don't refresh it before it falls off though because you will lose 1/3rd of it's damage.

If you have any other questions feel free to ask

-Guromin
