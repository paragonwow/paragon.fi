Hello,

4-Pc bonus should be around 300-400 dps upgrade depending on gear level both now and after the patch, so it definately better than any offpieces you might get. One mangle once every 30sec is not that big of a dps loss compared to 3% AP gain. Although it does make the "rotation" a bit annoying especially if you already have a manglebot. Poor itemization from Blizzard's part when comparing to other similar set bonuses, but that is nothing new.

If you want to look more closely how much of a upgrade 4-Pc is to you, I recommend http://sites.google.com/site/yawninglol/webmewpivot. It's very good cat calculator and fairly easy to use too.

Gear seems to be in order mostly, only in Cloak 22 Agi enchant from wotlk is actually better than 50 crit and in Hands 35 Str is better than 50 mastery. There is also 50 Str for Hands, but that is a expensive one. Oh, and for legs there is AP/Crit armor kit available. :)

Haven't heard about any haste cap and I doupt there is one apart from fairly theoretical cap, where you regen more energy than you can spend within a GCD. That is probably somewhere along several tens of thousands of haste rating. Certainly not relevant in this tier.

Hit doesn't really get better at 4.0.6. The DOT nerf/Mangle Shred buff just shifts damage more to the direction of direct yellow attacks (Mangle and Shred). But we still get 80% energy back per missed yellow attack, so relative value of hit stays the same. Haste gets a bit better though. Savage Roar stays the same too. Keep Rip up and after that SR.

Feral DPS do seem to drop once other classes get better gear and many of the heroics do favour ranged DPS quite a lot, so you might drop a few ranks, but if you already are in top 3, while interrupting I think you have nothing to fear. But Hunters, DKs, Rogues and Mages (and ofc boomkins) should top feral quite easily at similar gear levels once decked in 359/372 epics.

Hope this helps.

Primary stats (Agi, Str, Int etc.) got hugely buffed in Cata compared to ratings. Agi is the most desirable stat now by a large margin, but also Str isn't all that bad as we still get 2AP(+modifiers) per point of Str. Hands enchant is in a unique position as there is Str-, but no Agi-enchant available. Here are my stat-weights from Mew a couple weeks back:

DPS per point
Weapon DPS 3.92
Agi 2.83
Str 2.18
Mas 1.22
Cri 0.90
Has 0.87
Hit 0.80
Exp 0.80

As can be seen 35Str is 2.18*35 = 76DPS and 50Mas 1.22*50 = 61DPS. Same for cloak enchant, 22Agi beats 50Crit. It feels a little weird, but thats how it goes.

Xique wrote:I Cant really understand some Basics: how much increase the itemlvl the dmg Output ?
Mastery is a realy nice Thing but is agility really so much better ? How much dps bring's severage Roar more ? I have itemlvl 350 and make 14,5k dps in baradin hold at the pvp Boss. If i use sr how much dps more ?


Ilvl in itself doesn't increase dmg, but higher ilvl gear has more Agi and ratings, which leads to more dmg. How much more depends on the items in question as each of them have different ratings, but as a rule of thumb a higher ilvl item also increases your dmg output unless you are breaking an item set bonus.

Yes, Agi is just that much better. If you look the numbers above Agi is about 2.3 times as good as Mastery and 3.1 times as good as next rating Crit. Always go for Agi in your gemming and enchanting. SR increases your white hits by 50%. If you go to Character panel and multibly your Melee DPS by 0.5, it should give you rough estimate how much SR increases your DPS. I'm guessing around 800 DPS or so with your gear. But if SR and Rip are dropping at a same time, always refresh Rip first. SR is decent, but it's still a way behind Rip in damage done.
