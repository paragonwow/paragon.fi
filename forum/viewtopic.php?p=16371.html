Madness is a 2-3 (raid) night boss. It is fairly straightforward, but you still have to hit into the problems to fix them. Then once you have it down, you need to execute it without Thrall failing or someone disconnecting to meet the number requirements.

The final encounter being a relative pushover, this race was about Spine. It has some kinks you need to work around, but stripped down, it's about doing the maximum amount of damage in ~20 seconds (the cast is 23 seconds, but in practice it's visible at around 3 seconds elapsed). It requires a stacked comp and gear. A week's worth of gear is the difference between 'wiping' at 1 million or killing a tendon -- speaking from experience :) But of course gear and comp are two sides of the same coin. Killing Spine would not be a problem with something like this in bis Firelands/LFR gear.

I'm also aware of Stars' 8-openings tactic, but that only shifts focus away from one area in the comp to something else. You'll still have a very, very hard time doing that without gear and a stack of arcane mages, for example.

So all in all, KIN (and Stars) had the comp, the gear, and the execution. That 8 days meant we lacked one week of gear, and we weren't able to make up for it in the comp or execution. It's hard to speculate what would have happened if gear had been on equal footing; could we have matched the comp and execution, or would one or the other still have been lacking? We'll never know :)
arx / xaar

I think the biggest problem about Madness is that it lacks the generic fire for people to die in. There's very few chances for an individual player to wipe the raid or get themselves killed, and from beginning to the end it's mostly just switch target - dps - switch target - dps, which gets easy after a bit of practice when everyone's got the feel of when the switches are coming and how to deal with the efficiently. There's a few instances of "oh you have a debuff, why don't you stand over there" and spreading out but other than that people will only need to worry about hitting the right targets and using their cooldowns right. Madness is like a series of checkpoints and after Spine they all feel pretty tame.
