Posts: 551
The whelps blow up in around ~30 seconds with our dps (depends on whether we use lust or not). So I just tab through them, cast swp first and then start channeling vt and already tab away before the cast lands (this gives you time to see if your new targets has your dots on and decide to either tab away or recast something). Most likely I'd recast a vt with ~4 seconds left, and mf the target if it already has swp. When the whelps are about to die I make sure that at least my focus targets has dots (and if easy to pick, dot the drakes as well) and might throw in a mind sear to finish the whelps off.

I never "tab back" to find _that_ mob with 2 seconds of vt left when I tabbed away. Not worth it.

On Halfus you can use target macros too, it's really easy that way.

Take Halfus for your focus and make 4 target macros(/tar Storm/Time/Nether/Slate)

Always go in the same order, so non of your dots should drop.

Full dots on halfus -> /tar Storm, vt + swp -> /tar Time, vt + swp -> /tar Nether, vt + swp -> /tar Slate, vt + swp -> /tar focus(halfus), vt + mf -> /tar Storm, vt + mf -> /tar Time, vt + mf -> /tar Nether, vt + mf -> /tar Slate, vt + mf -- and repeat

You just need to cast your mb everytime it's rdy, cause you will almost always have 3 orbs, while are 5 targets are alive, you vt will drop till you come to the same targets again, but after 1 drake is down, you can keep all your dots rolling and don't forget to refresh DP sometimes, I keep DP on Halfus, cause it's much easier.
